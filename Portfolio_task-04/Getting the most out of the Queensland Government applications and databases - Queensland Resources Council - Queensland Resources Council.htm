Getting the most out of the Queensland Government applications and databases

Date:  27 August 2o19

Time:  9.00am – 10.30am

Venue:  QRC offices, Level 13, 133 Mary Street, Brisbane

 

BOOK NOW

QImagery – Is an online aerial photograph library that allows you to search for and view images by location. You can access more than 900,000 historical aerial photographs of Queensland from the 1930s to 2012.

https://qimagery.information.qld.gov.au/

Queensland Globe – Is a free online interactive mapping tool that allows you to access hundreds of spatial data layers on Queensland mining and exploration, land valuations, property and land parcels, natural resources, geology and more. You can create your own maps, add your own data, export data and login to save and share maps.

https://qldglobe.information.qld.gov.au/

QSpatial – The Queensland Spatial Catalogue is a geospatial portal that allows access to Queensland Government spatial data and information. You can search for downloadable datasets, pre-packaged datasets, live data and maps (web services) and spatially related applications.

http://qldspatial.information.qld.gov.au/

Geocoder – Use the Geocoder to transform a physical address into geographical coordinates or vice versa. For example you can use this geocode tool to find the:

• lot on plan of a Queensland address

• coordinates (latitude and longitude) of an address or lot on plan

• address using the lot on plan or coordinates.

https://geocode.information.qld.gov.au/

GeoResGlobe – a new spatial viewer to support the future of mining and exploration in Queensland.

https://georesglobe.information.qld.gov.au/
